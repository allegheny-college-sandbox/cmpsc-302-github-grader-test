Whole Wheat Moroccan Bread

The traditional recipe for Moroccan Khubz Maghrebi calls for all white flour. Although this works well, it imparts little flavor, so I've created a heartier whole wheat version.

  • TypeBreads
  • Prep20 min
  • Rise1 hour 30 min
  • Cook30 min
  • Total2 hour 30 min
  • Yields3 round loaves

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 ½ cups warm water (100° to 110°F)
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons instant yeast
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • cooking spray
  • 2 teaspoons whole wheat flour
  • cooking spray
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, dissolve yeast in water.
  2. In a small bowl, combine flours and salt.
  3. Pour in about ⅓ of the the flour into the mixing bowl with water.
  4. Mix well with a dough whisk or wooden spoon until incorporated.
  5. Pour in another ⅓ of the flour, stir, and repeat until incorporated.
  6. Stir well with a dough whisk until a rough ball of dough starts to forms.
  7. Turn out onto a clean work surface and knead by hand until dough is soft and smooth, about 8 to 10 minutes.

Rising

  1. Form dough into a round and place in a lightly greased bowl.
  2. Cover with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel and let dough rise until doubled, about 1 hour.
  3. By hand, gentle remove the dough from the bowl, press down, and form into a 12 inch cylinder.
  4. Using a pastry knife or bench scrapper, cut into three each pieces. Form each piece into a disc.
  5. Lightly grease a baking sheet and place all three discs onto it.
  6. Sprinkle each with whole wheat flour and cover.
  7. Let sit until dough has risen by half, about 40 minutes.

Baking

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F with rack at bottom third position.
  2. Remove plastic wrap from baking sheet and brush each with 1 teaspoon oil.
  3. Bake at 350°F until an instant-read thermometer inserted into center of loaf reads 190°F, about 30 to 35 minutes.
    • Note that the bread will only turn slightly golden brown.
  4. Remove from oven and transfer to a wire rack. Let cool completely to room temperature, about 2 to 3 hours.
  5. Slice and serve.
